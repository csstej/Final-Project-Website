Personality Configurations, NLP and Psychometrics

The intent of this project is to explore the relationship between personality categorization and response time to each survey item.   This aim is a compelling contribution to the literature on personality, due to current reliance on word association to determine personality. Understanding hesitancy, or lack thereof, in item response adds a physiological component to what we know of personality. We aim to improve survey questions based on NLP techniques and Factor analysis.

Our Work

Response time on survey items will be examined for outliers prior to cluster analysis to account for extraneous variables that may skew potential finding. A separate dataset of individuals’ self-selected personality types and their social media posts will give us the opportunity to employ natural language processing on open text, triangulating any existing relationship between dispositions and processing. Keywords can include the following: cautious, careful, mindful, hesitant, indecisive, reluctant, thoughtful. Alternatively, other groups of keywords can include decisive, confident, rash, determined, impulsive, spontaneous, hasty, and uninhibited. These and similar words and phrasing can indicate an individuals’ disposition towards time spent towards decisions and choices. Findings from this investigation will be cross examined against our initial quantitative analysis. 

Our Findings

Through the use of NLP and Sentiment Analysis on the user postings of the MBTI dataset, the identification of keywords with significant sentiment score is achieved. With these keywords, new survey statements can be constructed that are better tailored towards specific personality types.  

Conslusion

  • Improved capture and measure of personality traits. 
  • Aid in internationalizing surveys. 
  • Survey participant recruitment and retention
